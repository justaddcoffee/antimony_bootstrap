## 5. Summary and Recommendations

### Key Findings

1. **Neuroinflammation/TREM2 axis is strongly represented**: PMC6433910 (sTREM2) and PMC6417433 (TREM2 loss) provide mechanistic detail on TREM2's dual role in microglial Ab clearance and amyloid seeding regulation. These are high-priority for the neuroinflammation module.

2. **Cellular senescence emerges as a modeling target**: PMC6260915, PMC6206507, and PMC6605052 collectively describe tau-associated senescence, senescent cell clearance benefits, and Ab-driven OPC senescence. This could form a new senescence sub-module.

3. **Sleep-wake tau dynamics (PMC6410369)**: Provides a clear activity-dependent tau release mechanism that maps directly to an ODE (tau_ISF production proportional to neuronal activity, first-order clearance to CSF).

4. **ApoE-complement interaction (PMC6420126)**: ApoE complexes with activated C1q, providing a direct molecular mechanism linking ApoE genotype to inflammation resolution.

5. **Novel APP processing via eta-secretase (PMC6570618)**: Adds an alternative APP cleavage pathway generating Aeta-alpha, which inhibits LTP. Extends the APP processing module beyond classical alpha/beta/gamma secretase pathways.

6. **BBB and clearance mechanisms**: Multiple papers (PMC6410369, PMC6689199, PMC6148759) inform transport and clearance reactions at the BBB and through glymphatic/perivascular routes.
